The Alluring Wilderness of Chitral Valley: Beyond the Known Paths

By Asmar Hussain · 22 September 2023

In the northern reaches of Pakistan lies a hidden gem that has remained relatively untouched and unexplored by the masses. The Chitral Valley, a place of breathtaking beauty and warm hospitality, beckons travelers to experience its unique charm. I have had the privilege of visiting this enchanting valley for nearly 6-7 years now, and with each visit, I find myself more captivated by its pristine landscapes and the welcoming nature of its inhabitants.

A Journey to Remember

Traveling to Chitral has always been a journey of discovery. For many years, the bad road conditions made it a less frequented destination. However, the recent completion of the Lowari Tunnel has significantly eased access to this picturesque valley, making it a convenient trip from cities like Islamabad and Peshawar. This improvement has opened up new opportunities for tourists to explore the valley’s wonders without the hassles of a difficult journey.

A Geographical Marvel

Chitral holds a key geographical position, nestled amidst towering mountains and lush green valleys. One of its standout features is the amazing Wakhan Corridor, a narrow strip of land that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and historical significance. The region is also home to several national parks and dozens of lakes, where nature enthusiasts can witness the diverse flora and fauna that inhabit the area.

The Kalash Valleys: A Cultural Experience

A visit to Chitral would be incomplete without exploring the Kalash Valleys. These valleys are home to the Kalash people, an ethnic group known for their distinctive culture and traditions. As you wander through these valleys, you’ll be greeted with vibrant colors, unique architectural styles, and the warm smiles of the Kalash people, making it a truly unforgettable experience. I have been traveling to Kalash with a cause to promote this culture. These innocent kids are my friends. I love to take gifts (stationery items) with me whenever I travel to bamborait and distribute them among students.

Shandur Top: The Roof of the World

For adventure seekers, a trip to Shandur Top is a must. Often referred to as the ‘Roof of the World’, this place offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is also the venue for the annual Shandur Polo Festival, where teams from Chitral and Gilgit-Baltistan come together for Polo matches amidst the high mountains, a spectacle that attracts visitors from all over the world.

A Photographer’s Paradise

As someone who has been photographing this place for several years, I can attest to the fact that Chitral is a paradise for photography enthusiasts. The valley offers a plethora of photographic opportunities, from majestic mountains to serene rivers and vibrant local cultures. My journey in promoting the tourism of this valley through my photographs has been a fulfilling experience, and I encourage others to do the same.

Hospitality at its Best

Chitral is not just about scenic beauty; it is also a place where visitors can experience genuine hospitality. The locals are known for their loving and peaceful nature, always ready to welcome tourists with open arms. Their kindness adds to the overall charm of the valley, making your visit a heartwarming experience.

Let’s Contribute to Chitral’s Tourism

As we step into a new era of accessibility and awareness, let us all contribute more to the tourism sector of Chitral. By visiting this valley, not only do we get to witness its untouched beauty, but we also help in promoting local businesses and communities. Together, we can help Chitral flourish as a prime tourist destination, bringing prosperity to its people and preserving its natural and cultural heritage for generations to come.

In conclusion, the Chitral Valley is a place where nature and culture come together to offer a unique and enriching experience. I invite you to visit this beautiful destination and discover its many wonders firsthand. Let’s embark on a journey of discovery and appreciation, and let the beauty of Chitral captivate your heart and soul.

By ASMAR HUSSAIN, an award winning landscape photographer
